\section{Expected Outcomes}

We expect to produce a garment folding policy that outperforms the RGB-based competition baselines on unseen garment styles. Our primary claim is that point cloud representations generalize better than RGB for cross-style garment folding, extending DP3's demonstrated advantage from rigid objects to deformable garments. Our secondary claim is that NOCS normalization provides additional gains on top of raw point clouds by mapping cross-garment geometric variation into a shared canonical space, building on UniFolding's evidence for this mechanism in a different action framework.
