\section{Visuomotor Framework}
\label{sec:method}

In this section, we formally describe the architectures and representations employed in our study of bimanual garment folding. Our framework, illustrated in the system architecture diagram in Figure \ref{fig:system_diagram}, integrates generative temporal modeling with high-capacity visual backbones and mid-level geometric priors.

\subsection{Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT)}
We employ Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT) \cite{zhao2023act}, which mitigates compounding errors in behavioral cloning by predicting action sequences $a_{t:t+k}$ given an observation $o_t$. The architecture uses a Conditional Variational Autoencoder (CVAE) to model trajectory "style" via a latent variable $z$, and a Transformer decoder to predict the action chunk $\hat{a}_{t:t+k}$. We use a default chunk size $k=100$. While standard ACT operates open-loop ($n_{action}=k$), we utilize a closed-loop reactive protocol ($n_{action}=1$) for our best configurations, enabling re-planning at every timestep to handle the complex dynamics of textile manipulation.

\subsection{Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Architectures}
We evaluate several Vision-Language-Action models, including SmolVLA \cite{smolvla2025}, $\pi_{0.5}$ \cite{pi05_2026}, and X-VLA \cite{zheng2025xvlasoftpromptedtransformerscalable}. These models utilize large-scale pre-trained Vision-Language Models (VLMs) as visual encoders, which are integrated with action-prediction heads. To adapt these massive backbones to the specialized domain of garment folding, we employ Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) via Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) \cite{hu2021lora}. LoRA allows for the optimization of a small subset of parameters by introducing low-rank decomposition matrices into the attention layers, thereby preventing catastrophic forgetting while enabling task-specific alignment.

\subsection{Dataset and Evaluation Protocol}
The study utilizes the LeHome Challenge dataset \cite{lehome2026}, which comprises 12 unique garments per category (long-sleeved tops, short-sleeved tops, long pants, and shorts), for a total of 48 initial garments provided by the organizers. Our training pipeline utilizes 10 garments from each category, while the remaining 2 garments per category are held out for local unseen evaluation. 

The final performance is validated through two distinct protocols:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Local Evaluation}: Conducted on the 10 seen and 2 unseen garments per category provided in the simulation environment. Each model is evaluated on 10 episodes per garment, with each episode initialized with stochastic rotation and translation offsets to ensure robust testing.
    \item \textbf{Leaderboard Evaluation}: The models are submitted for evaluation on a comprehensive set of 20 unique garments per category (80 total), including secret variations.
\end{itemize}

A successful fold is defined by the final geometric configuration of the garment at the end of a 20-second episode. Specifically, success is registered when the Euclidean distance between predefined keypoints on the garment (e.g., the distance between $P_1$ and $P_3$) satisfies a task-specific threshold (e.g., $\le 6.7$ cm), ensuring that the garment is aligned according to the target fold pattern.


\subsection{Visual Representations and Backbones}
Our framework leverages diverse visual backbones and mid-level representations to reduce appearance-based overfitting:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{ResNet-18}: We utilize ResNet-18 \cite{he2015deepresiduallearningimage} as our primary visual backbone. While lower in capacity than modern vision transformers, its relative simplicity and inductive biases provide superior generalization to unseen garment styles when trained on our domain-specific dataset.
    \item \textbf{Colorized Depth}: To provide explicit geometric priors, we augment RGB observations with colorized depth maps. Raw depth values are mapped to a 3-channel JET-style gradient, allowing the visual encoders to leverage pre-trained features while perceiving the 3D structure of folds and wrinkles.
    \item \textbf{Data Augmentations}: During training, we apply a suite of stochastic augmentations, including random cropping and rotation, to both RGB and colorized depth inputs. This regularizes the model and improves generalization to novel garment styles and poses.
\end{itemize}
