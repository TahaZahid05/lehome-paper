\begin{abstract}
Robotic manipulation of highly deformable garments presents significant challenges due to their high dimensionality and complex dynamics. This paper presents an exploratory study of diverse imitation learning strategies within the context of the ICRA 2026 LeHome Challenge. We investigate a progression of architectures and visuomotor policies, evolving from Diffusion Policy through a sequence of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models—including SmolVLA, $\pi_{0.5}$, and X-VLA—ultimately converging on Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT). Our study systematically evaluates the impact of mid-level geometric priors (colorized depth), robust data augmentations, and Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT). We further analyze the effects of critical hyperparameters such as action chunk size, execution steps, and the integration of a DINOv2 visual backbone. Our results demonstrate that while VLA-based models show strong semantic understanding, they are limited by the scale of the demonstration dataset. In contrast, ACT provides the data efficiency and fine-grained coordination required for bimanual garment folding. We report per-garment success rates, showing significant performance gains across categories, with a final macro-average success rate of 63.54\% and enhanced generalization to unseen garment styles.
\end{abstract}
